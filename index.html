📈 Periodic Trends — Definitions & Diagrams

📏 Atomic Radius Physical

Definition: Half the distance between the nuclei of two covalently bonded atoms of the same element.

Measures atom size. Larger radius = larger atom.

📊 Trend Direction

Period→
Group↓
DECREASES →
INCREASES ↓
Smallest: Top-right
Largest: Bottom-left

Atomic Radius Across Period 2 (pm)

→ Across DECREASES
  • More protons (+1 each element)
  • Electrons in same shell
  • Stronger nuclear pull → smaller
↓ Down INCREASES
  • New electron shell added
  • Electrons further from nucleus
  • Greater shielding → larger

⚡ First Ionisation Energy Chemical

Definition: Energy required to remove one mole of electrons from one mole of gaseous atoms to form one mole of singly charged gaseous cations.
X(g) → X⁺(g) + e⁻

📊 Trend Direction

Period→
Group↓
INCREASES →
DECREASES ↓
Highest: He (top-right)
Lowest: Fr (bottom-left)

First IE Across Period 2 (kJ/mol)

→ Across INCREASES
  • More protons → stronger hold
  • Same number of shells
  • Electron is removed less readily
↓ Down DECREASES
  • Electron further from nucleus
  • Greater shielding
  • Electron is removed more readily
💡 Link: Lower IE → more reactive metals. K has lower IE than Na, so K is more reactive!

🧲 Electronegativity Chemical

Definition: Ability of an atom to attract shared electrons in a covalent bond.

Fluorine is most electronegative (4.0).

📊 Trend Direction

Period→
Group↓
INCREASES →
DECREASES ↓
Highest: F (4.0)
Lowest: Fr (~0.7)

Electronegativity Period 2 (Pauling)

🔄 Displacement Reactions

🔄 What is Displacement?

A more reactive element displaces a less reactive element from its compound.

Rule: A more reactive halogen displaces a less reactive halide. Order: Cl₂ > Br₂ > I₂

Chlorine + Potassium Bromide

✅ Reacts
Chemical Equation:
Cl₂(aq) + 2KBr(aq) → 2KCl(aq) + Br₂(aq)
Ionic Equation:
Cl₂(aq) + 2Br⁻(aq) → 2Cl⁻(aq) + Br₂(aq)
Reduction: Cl₂ + 2e⁻ → 2Cl⁻
Oxidation: 2Br⁻ → Br₂ + 2e⁻
🔍 Observation:
  • Colourless → orange/brown
  • Bromine produced

Chlorine + Potassium Iodide

✅ Reacts
Chemical Equation:
Cl₂(aq) + 2KI(aq) → 2KCl(aq) + I₂(aq)
Ionic Equation:
Cl₂(aq) + 2I⁻(aq) → 2Cl⁻(aq) + I₂(aq)
🔍 Observation: Colourless → brown (iodine)

Bromine + Potassium Iodide

✅ Reacts
Chemical Equation:
Br₂(aq) + 2KI(aq) → 2KBr(aq) + I₂(aq)
Ionic Equation:
Br₂(aq) + 2I⁻(aq) → 2Br⁻(aq) + I₂(aq)
🔍 Observation: Orange → darker brown

Bromine + Potassium Chloride

❌ No Reaction
Br₂(aq) + 2KCl(aq) → No Reaction
Why? Br is less reactive than Cl. Cannot displace Cl⁻.

📋 Halogen Displacement Summary

Halogen ↓+ KCl+ KBr+ KI
Cl₂—✅ Orange✅ Brown
Br₂❌—✅ Brown
I₂❌❌—

Rule: A more reactive metal displaces a less reactive metal from its salt solution.

Zinc + Copper(II) Sulfate

✅ Reacts
Chemical Equation:
Zn(s) + CuSO₄(aq) → ZnSO₄(aq) + Cu(s)
Ionic Equation:
Zn(s) + Cu²⁺(aq) → Zn²⁺(aq) + Cu(s)
Oxidation: Zn → Zn²⁺ + 2e⁻
Reduction: Cu²⁺ + 2e⁻ → Cu
🔍 Observations:
  • Blue solution → colourless
  • Brown copper deposited on zinc
  • Solution warms up

Iron + Copper(II) Sulfate

✅ Reacts
Chemical Equation:
Fe(s) + CuSO₄(aq) → FeSO₄(aq) + Cu(s)
Ionic Equation:
Fe(s) + Cu²⁺(aq) → Fe²⁺(aq) + Cu(s)
🔍 Observations:
  • Blue → light green (Fe²⁺ formed)
  • Brown copper deposited

Magnesium + Copper(II) Sulfate

✅ Very Vigorous!
Chemical Equation:
Mg(s) + CuSO₄(aq) → MgSO₄(aq) + Cu(s)
Ionic Equation:
Mg(s) + Cu²⁺(aq) → Mg²⁺(aq) + Cu(s)
🔍 Observations:
  • Vigorous reaction, solution gets hot
  • Blue → colourless rapidly
  • Brown copper deposited

Copper + Zinc Sulfate

❌ No Reaction
Cu(s) + ZnSO₄(aq) → No Reaction
Why? Cu is less reactive than Zn. Cannot displace Zn²⁺.

📋 Metal Displacement Summary

Metal ↓+ MgSO₄+ ZnSO₄+ FeSO₄+ CuSO₄
Mg—✅✅✅
Zn❌—✅✅
Fe❌❌—✅
Cu❌❌❌—

🔄 Understanding Redox (OIL RIG)

Oxidation

  • Oxidation Is Loss of electrons
  • Increase in oxidation state
Zn → Zn²⁺ + 2e⁻

Reduction

  • Reduction Is Gain of electrons
  • Decrease in oxidation state
Cu²⁺ + 2e⁻ → Cu
